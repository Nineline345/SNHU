**Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?**
The client was The Gaming Room, and they wanted to move their game Draw It or Lose It from a single Android app to a web-based application that works across multiple platforms. The software needed to support multiple games, teams, and players, while also ensuring unique IDs and names with a centralized service managing the data

**What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?**
I did well in clearly organizing the system design and explaining how the classes and relationships work together. The use of inheritance, a service layer, and design patterns like singleton made the structure easy to follow and scalable.

**What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?**
Working through the design document helped me plan everything before coding. It made it easier to understand how objects relate to each other and prevented confusion later when implementing the actual logic.

**If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?**
I would improve the system architecture section by adding a more detailed breakdown of how components communicate. This would make the design clearer, especially for understanding how the system works in a distributed environment.

**How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?**
I focused on the requirement for cross-platform support and designed the system as a web-based service with a centralized backend. Considering user needs is important because the software has to actually work for the client’s goals, not just function technically.

**How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?**
I approached the design by breaking the system into smaller parts, defining relationships, and applying object-oriented principles. In the future, I would continue using UML diagrams, clear service layers, and planning documentation first before writing code.
